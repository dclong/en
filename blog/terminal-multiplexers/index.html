Terminal Multiplexers

Sep 04, 2021

  1. There are 2 mature popular terminal multiplexer apps: screen and tmux. Both of them are very useful if you want to work on multiple tasks over 1 SSH connection. Screen is relative simple to use while tmux is much more powerful and more complicated to use.

  2. Besides enabling users to work with multiple tasks over 1 SSH connection, screen and tmux also prevents running tasks from being killed on interruption of SSH connection. Another way to prevent a long-running task from being killed on interruption of SSH connection is to use nohup and &. nohup tells the server not to kill your task when SSH is disconnect and & place your job to background (so that you can work on other tasks). nohup + & is simple but only works for non-interactive jobs. 

    nohup your_command_to_run &

  3. Zellij is a new terminal multiplexer implemented in Rust. It is functionally very similar to tmux but with batteries included and is easier to extend via its plugin system back by WASM. It is also more intuitive to use. Overall, I recommend Zellij rather than screen or tmux.
